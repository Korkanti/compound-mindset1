About Managed Funds

What is a Managed Fund?

A managed fund is a pool of money collected from multiple investors to invest in a diversified portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other assets. It is managed by professional fund managers who aim to achieve the fund’s investment objective on behalf of its investors.

How Does it Work?

When you invest in a managed fund, you buy units or shares of that fund. Each unit represents your portion of ownership in the fund’s portfolio. The value of your investment grows (or shrinks) based on the performance of the underlying assets.

Types of managed Funds

  • Equity Funds: Invest primarily in stocks for long-term growth.
  • Bond Funds: Invest in fixed-income securities like government or corporate bonds.
  • Balanced Funds: Mix of stocks and bonds for moderate growth and income.
  • Index Funds: Mirror the performance of a specific market index (e.g., ASX 200).
  • Money Market Funds: Invest in short-term, low-risk securities for capital preservation.

Understanding Market Capitalisation

Market capitalisation (market cap) refers to the total value of a company’s outstanding shares. It is used to classify companies into:

  • Large-Cap: Well-established companies with stable performance (e.g., Commonwealth Bank).
  • Mid-Cap: Medium-sized companies with potential for growth.
  • Small-Cap: Smaller companies that may offer higher returns with higher risk.

Managed funds may focus on different market cap segments depending on their investment strategy.

Benefits of Investing in Managed Funds

  • Diversification to reduce risk.
  • Professional management of funds.
  • Accessibility with small amounts of capital.
  • Liquidity — you can usually buy/sell units on demand.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is my money locked in?
It depends on the type of fund. Some funds allow withdrawals anytime; others (like retirement funds) may have restrictions.
What are expense ratios?
Expense ratio is the annual fee a fund charges its investors, expressed as a percentage of assets under management.
Can managed funds guarantee returns?
No. Managed funds are subject to market risks, and returns are not guaranteed.
